Walk Smart

For individuals who live in densely packed urban communities, safe while walking home alone at night is a prominent concern. This Android Application, Walk Smart aims to help individuals feel safe while walking home alone at night by automatically contacting your selected friends to let them know that you are about to depart for a next destination.

What makes this app unique? It's a two tier safety based on individual action and social circle engagement.It automatically sends selected contacts a preset notification telling them when you will start walking home, while keeping track of the duration of your walk. If you do not signal that you have arrived home before the timer ends, there will be a message that pops up asking about your whereabouts. If you do not disable the message within a certain timeframe, the app will automatically send messages to your selected contacts. Still feeling uneasy on your walk? There's a help button to send more texts to your friends alerting them to call you.

  • Collaborators: Faye Feng, Shelley Shen, Jenevive Ayuste
  • Date: Feburary 2015
  • Project Type: Android App

Autonmous Candle Detection Robot

The presented request for proposal delineated a manufacturer’s demand for an autonomous, portable machine that was able to check the functionality of a specific model of LED candlelights. Amongst other constraints discussed further in the report, the machine was required to be tray-loaded, capable of accurately test up to nine candlelights, and able to complete its operation within a 90 second time frame. The status of each candlelight’s functionality was to be obtained from the machine via an intuitive keypad and LCD interface. As a solution to this proposed problem, the EOS Cube, a proof-of-concept machine, was designed and manufactured at a cost of $201.10 CDN.

The EOS Cube featured a removable square tray that could be inserted into a robust, symmetrical chassis. A precise switch mechanism simultaneously turned on all of the candles in the tray, and a stationary sensor array situated above the candles worked in conjunction with the microcontroller to discern the particular functionality classification of each candle.

  • Collaborators: Isabella Huang, Max Wei
  • Date: April 2014
  • Project Type: Robotics
